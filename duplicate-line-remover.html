Every option, explained

Six options for precise control over how duplicates are detected and what the output looks like.

🔡 Case-insensitive Match

Treats "Apple", "apple", and "APPLE" as the same line so they all collapse into one, regardless of capitalisation differences.

⎵ Trim Whitespace

Strips leading and trailing spaces from each line before comparing, so " banana" and "banana " are correctly treated as identical.

⬜ Remove Blank Lines

Drops empty or whitespace-only lines from the output entirely, giving you a compact, gap-free result list.

🔼 Sort A → Z

After deduplication, sorts all unique lines into ascending alphabetical order for easy scanning or further processing.

🔽 Sort Z → A

Sorts all unique lines in descending alphabetical order. Mutually exclusive with A → Z — the last toggle you activated wins.

⬇ Keep Last Occurrence

By default the tool keeps the first occurrence of a duplicate. Enable this to keep the last one instead — useful for versioned lists.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is this tool free to use?▾

Yes, 100% free. No account, no subscription, no usage limits. All TextCraft tools are free forever.

Does my data get uploaded anywhere?▾

No. Every operation runs entirely in your browser using JavaScript. Your list is never sent to a server, stored, or logged anywhere.

What counts as a "duplicate" line?▾

By default, any line that appears more than once is a duplicate. With "Case-insensitive" enabled, "Apple" and "apple" count as the same. With "Trim whitespace" enabled, leading and trailing spaces are ignored before comparing.

Can I use Sort A→Z and Sort Z→A at the same time?▾

No — only one sort direction applies at a time. If both are toggled on, the last one you activated takes effect and the other is ignored.

Is there a line or character limit?▾

No hard limit. Processing runs locally so capacity depends on your device. In practice, lists of hundreds of thousands of lines work without any problem on a modern computer.

What's the difference between "Keep first" and "Keep last"?▾

When a line appears multiple times, "Keep first" (the default) retains its earliest position in the list. "Keep last occurrence" retains its final position instead. This matters when line order carries meaning, such as in versioned or prioritised lists.
